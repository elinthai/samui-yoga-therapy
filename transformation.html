What the data tracks

Every scan captures 400+ measurements. Here's what twenty sessions of consistent work moves.

Posture

How you hold yourself at rest — forward head, lateral tilt, anterior pelvic tilt. The scan sees what the mirror misses.

Range of motion

What opens and what stays closed. Where you compensate. What's been guarded for years and has started to release.

Body composition

Fat percentage, lean mass distribution, circumference at every point. Not weight — what the weight is made of and where it lives.

Symmetry

Left-right imbalances in muscle development and joint loading. The kind of asymmetry that causes injury when left unaddressed.

Breath capacity

How deeply the body breathes when the nervous system trusts the room. Tracked session to session, not just scan to scan.

Nervous system indicators

Resting tension, recovery speed, the baseline the body returns to after stress. Harder to name. Visible in the practice.
