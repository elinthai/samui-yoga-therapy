## About

**Body copy:**
I came to this practice the hard way. I joined the US military at eighteen and came home with injuries I couldn't always see — the kind that end a career early. What rebuilt me wasn't a programme. It was breath, movement, and stillness: massage, yoga, and meditation, practised first out of need and kept because they worked.

I trained as a licensed massage therapist, then as a yoga teacher, and eventually followed the practice to Koh Samui — where I've made my home with my family, and where I study Thai Buddhist philosophy alongside the monks of Wat Teepangkorn.

Samui Yoga Therapy is the studio I wish I'd had: small, quiet, personal. I teach one person — or a few — at a time, meeting each body where it is that day, and walking with you as the practice slowly becomes part of your life.

Wherever you are on the path, there's a place for you on this floor. มีที่สำหรับคุณที่นี่.

## Services

**Intro line:**
The studio is intimate by design — so everything here is personal. You don't book a style. You book a practice, and we shape it to you.

### Private sessions — one to one
The heart of the studio. Each session is built for the body and mind you bring that day:

- Vinyasa & Power — strength, mobility, flowing movement
- Yin — deep release, recovery, slowing down
- Ashtanga — traditional, progressive, disciplined
- Advanced asana — for the committed practitioner going deeper
- Breathwork & meditation — steadiness for the nervous system
- Strength & mobility — practical, functional, resilient

### Practice together — semi-private (2–3)
The same close attention, shared with a partner or a couple of friends.

### Sound healing — small group (up to 5)
The one gathering the studio holds. A still hour of vibration and rest, by reservation.

### What we work on together
Stress and burnout · injury recovery and mobility · sleep and rest · building strength · beginning a practice from zero · deepening one you've had for years.
