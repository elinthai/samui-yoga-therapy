About Eli

I came to this practice the hard way. I joined the US military at eighteen and came home with injuries I couldn't always see — the kind that end a career early. What rebuilt me wasn't a programme. It was breath, movement, and stillness: massage, yoga, and meditation, practised first out of need and kept because they worked.

I trained as a licensed massage therapist, then as a yoga teacher, and eventually followed the practice to Koh Samui — where I've made my home with my family, and where I study Thai Buddhist philosophy alongside the monks of Wat Teepangkorn.

Samui Yoga Therapy exists because I needed it and it didn't exist — small, quiet, built around one person at a time. That person is you.

Over almost four years, I tracked 115 body scans on myself — posture, range of motion, breath capacity, nervous system indicators — and used that data to refine every modality I teach. That research wasn't for a certificate. It was the proof I needed to trust what I was offering. When I work with you, that same data-trained eye is in the room: reading how you hold yourself, where you are guarding, what is ready to open.

Common questions

What is yoga therapy?
Yoga therapy uses movement, breath, and awareness practices tailored to the individual — not to a group or a style. Sessions are built around your body, your history, and what you need that day.
Do I need any experience?
No. Many clients come with no yoga background at all. The sessions are shaped to where you are, not to a level you're expected to reach.
How long is a session?
Private sessions run 90 minutes. The first session includes a brief intake conversation, built into that time.
Where is the studio?
The studio is in Bophut, Koh Samui. The full address is sent when you book — it's a quiet spot, by appointment.
What should I bring?
Comfortable clothing and water. The studio provides mats, props, and everything else. Just arrive.
