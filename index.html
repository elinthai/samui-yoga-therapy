About Eli

I came to this practice the hard way. I joined the US military at eighteen and came home with injuries I couldn't always see — the kind that end a career early. What rebuilt me wasn't a programme. It was breath, movement, and stillness: massage, yoga, and meditation, practised first out of need and kept because they worked.

I trained as a licensed massage therapist, then as a yoga teacher, and eventually followed the practice to Koh Samui — where I've made my home with my family, and where I study Thai Buddhist philosophy alongside the monks of Wat Teepangkorn.

Samui Yoga Therapy is the studio I wish I'd had: small, quiet, personal. I teach one person — or a few — at a time, meeting each body where it is that day, and walking with you as the practice slowly becomes part of your life.
